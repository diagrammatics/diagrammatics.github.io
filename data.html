So... what to learn? How to go about it? It seems that the spot to start is at the beginning.

Here is a (hand-picked) list of entry-level specializations in Data Analysis on Coursera:

IBM - Data Analyst Professional Certificate↗︎
4 months at 10 hours/week
Python heavy, Excel, Cognos, SQL

Google - Data Analytics Professional Certificate↗︎
6 months at 10 hours/week
R heavy, Tableau as well

IBM - Data Analytics with Excel and R Professional Certificate↗︎
identical to the Python Version, except with R
3 months at 10 hours/week(why shorter?)
thinking I can potentially supplement?

UC Davis - Data Visualization with Tableau Specialization↗︎
this is all Tableau
1 month at 10 hours/week

That seems like a pretty good base. From there, it seems like the next step would be to supplement with various options if I feel I've not got a good enough picture. I think it also makes sense to direct my attention towards building a portfolio.

Here are some ideas for supplements:

Excel to MySQL: Analytic Techniques for Business Specialization↗︎
6 months at 5 hours/week
Excel and SQL, portfolio project?

Google Business Intelligence Professional Certificate↗︎
could complement analytics curriculum nicely
2 months at 10 hours/week

I've made a few initial choices which is to prioritize Tableau over Power BI (because I'm not sure I want to get into the Azure toolchain, though I'm sure there's some sort of quickstart for those learning), and at this point, I still can't tell whether or not I'm going to prioritize Python over R (though I'm leaning that way), as it seems that both are quite standard tools in analytics.

As it stands, that 560 hours for the foundational coursework (assuming the IBM R course has no overlap), and 200 hours for the supplements. So if I want to get all that done in six months... that's about... 30 hours a week? Yikes. Even just the Google (R) and IBM (Python) courses alone are 400 hours combined which is still 16 hours per week. That's alot. That's a non-negligible amount of labor to put into something like this. And I've got to maintain things as I go. Woah.

Another aspect of data analysis is platform-specific knowledge, and there's many platforms, but outside of the Excel/Python/R/SQL stack above, there is some glaring holes (as of 2023). Here are a few other specializations that show some promise in terms of platform:

Microsoft Power BI Data Analyst Professional Certificate↗︎"
This is the biggest thing missing from the above, Microsoft's visualization suite, alternative to Tableau
5 months at 10 hours/week

SAS Programming Professional Certificate↗︎
SAS is another widely used analytics platform
2 months at 10 hours/week

UPenn - Business Analytics Specialization↗︎
Also seems like an interesting (soft) complement
1 month at 10 hours/week; pretty quick

There's another 320 hours on the secondary supplemental work.

Not that data science is the goal, because I'm under the impression that you need a degree to get into it, but here are some crunchier specializations in data science that may help round out some theoretical understanding and modeling skills(I think):

IBM - Data Science Professional Certificate↗︎
Seems like a good overview of DS, applied python, potential portfolio project?

Hopkins - Data Science Specialization↗︎
the crunchiest listed, R-based, may require stats
